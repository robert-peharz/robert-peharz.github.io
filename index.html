Short Bio

Robert Peharz received his PhD from the SPSC Lab, TU Graz (Austria) in 2015, where he was working on probabilistic graphical models and probabilistic circuits (sum-product networks), with applications to signal processing. He was a postdoc in the iDN group at the Medical University of Graz (Austria), working on interdisciplinary approaches for early recognition of neural maldevelopment via behavioral neuroscience. From 2017-2018 he was postdoc in the Machine Learning Group (MLG) at the University of Cambridge and a Marie-Curie Individual Fellow at MLG Cambridge from 2018-2019. Since 2019 he is an Assistant Professor in the Uncertainty in Artificialy Intelligence (UAI) at the Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e, Netherlands). Robert's interest are probabilistic machine learning, with a particular focus on principled and tractable inference in artificial intelligence.

ICML'20 Einsum Networks: Fast and Scalable Learning of Tractable Probabilistic Circuits
Robert Peharz, Steven Lang, Antonio Vergari, Karl Stelzner, Alejandro Molina, Martin Trapp, Guy Van Den Broeck, Kristian Kersting, Zoubin Ghahramani

Probabilistic circuits are tractable probabilistic models and allow exact and efficient inference. However, they used to be slow in comparison to deep neural networks. The reason is that their special structure (which make them tractable in the first place) is not really suitable for deep learning frameworks such as PyTorch or Tensorflow. Here we proposed a "smart" implementation of PCs, by squeezing all PC operations into one a handful large einsum operations. The result: dramatic speedups and memory savings (similar to other deep learning models), large-scale generative modeling, including data imputation and outlier detection.
